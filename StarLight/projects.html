Our Projects

Here are some of the projects that we have done with the Sri Lankan leading network companies.

National Backbone Network

This project aimed to provide a high-speed fiber optic network throughout the country, ensuring seamless connectivity.

Colombo City Wi-Fi

An initiative to provide free Wi-Fi access in various parts of the Colombo city was taken up by several telecom operators.

Undersea Cable Systems

Telecommunication companies have been part of international consortiums for undersea cable systems to boost international bandwidth and connectivity.

Mobile Money and e-Wallet Solutions

Dialog Axiata introduced "eZ Cash", a mobile money platform, enabling digital transactions and payments across the country.

Smart City Initiatives

Telecom companies have been part of various smart city projects to integrate ICT solutions for urban development.

E-Education Platforms

Telecommunication companies have introduced or partnered with platforms for online learning, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Telecom API Platforms

Dialog Axiata introduced Ideamart, a platform that allows developers to use telecom-based APIs to create applications, services, and solutions.

IoT Initiatives

everal Sri Lankan telecom companies have invested in IoT (Internet of Things) solutions for different industries, including agriculture, transportation, and healthcare.

Disaster Response Solutions

Given Sri Lanka's vulnerability to natural disasters, telecom companies have initiated disaster response solutions.

Network Expansion in Rural Areas

Many telecom companies in Sri Lanka have taken steps to extend their network coverage to remote and rural areas, ensuring that even the most far-flung areas of the country have access to communication services.

Cloud Services

Telecom operators have ventured into providing cloud services for enterprises, allowing businesses to operate more efficiently in a digitized environment.

4G/LTE Network Rollouts

Sri Lankan telecommunication companies, such as Dialog Axiata, Mobitel, and Hutch, have rolled out 4G networks across the country to provide high-speed internet access.
